How to Obtain a Group DOI for Publications

Researchers who wish to cite a group of related datasets in a publication can request a Group DOI (Digital Object Identifier) from the Brain Image Library. A Group DOI provides a single, citable link that resoves to a collection of datasets, ideal for journal publications.

Requirements

Before requesting a Group DOI, ensure that:

  • Each dataset you want to include is already uploaded and publicly available in the Brain Image Library
  • You have the BIL dataset IDs for all datasets to be grouped
  • You can provide basic publication and project details

How to Request a Group DOI

  1. Prepare the Group DOI Metadata
    • Download the Group DOI Metadata Sheet from: https://github.com/brain-image-library/group_doi_metadata/blob/main/BILGroupDOIMetadata.xlsx
    • Collect the BIL dataset identifiers or dataset URLs that you want to group together and include them as Related Identifiers on the Publications Tab.
    • Be sure to fill out all required fields including:
      • Title: A short descriptive title that will appear on the landing describing the data included
      • Abstract: A description of the data and background needed to interpret them.
      • Contributors: ORCID is required for all contributors listed
      • Funders: required for all NIH projects
  2. Send your Request to the BIL Team
    • Email your request with the Group DOI Metadata Sheet to bil-support@psc.edu
  3. Recieve Confirmation and DOI
    • The BIL team will respond with confirmation and provide you with the unique DOI that you can include in your publication.

After Publication

Once your paper is published, you can email BIL again to update the Group DOI landing page with a link to the publication if it was not included initially.

Questions

For assistance or more information, please contact the Brain Image Library team at bil-support@psc.edu.
